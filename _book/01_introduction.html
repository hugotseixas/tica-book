1  Introduction

The suppression of native vegetation in the Amazon and Cerrado biomes is a matter of great concern due to its potential impact on the environment and human well-being. There is a growing international interest in the protection of natural vegetation, in order to avoid greenhouse gases emissions and biodiversity loss. Understanding the drivers and effects of this phenomena is crucial to guide policies to avoid further natural vegetation suppression and mitigate negative impacts. However, this is a challenging task since the factors involved in this phenomenon are diverse, heterogeneous, and can even be contradictory. Existing literature suggests that native vegetation suppression is caused by economic fluctuations (Hargrave and Kis-Katos 2012), conservation policies (Pfaff et al. 2015), population distribution (Jusys 2016), land tenure (Cattaneo 2001), livestock and crop productions (Bowman 2016). On the other side, vegetation removal may impact human health (Santos and Almeida 2018), climate regime (Silva, Lopes, and Santos 2023), regional hydrology (Cavalcante et al. 2019), soil properties (Dias et al. 2022) and biodiversity (Gestich et al. 2021).

Unfortunately, much of the research in this area tends to focus on only a few dimensions of the problem, rather than taking a holistic approach. This narrow focus often fails to capture the full extent of the issue and can lead to oversimplifications or misdirection in efforts to solve it.

This project aims to predict and understand the causes and impacts of native vegetation suppression in the Cerrado and Amazon biomes, considering environmental and social dimensions. A mixed method will be employed, combining statistical regression and classification algorithms, and sociological analysis. The results will provide a deeper understanding of the relationships between land use changes, environmental factors, and social dynamics related to native vegetation suppression.

The results of this project will contribute to a better understanding of the causes and impacts of native vegetation suppression in the Cerrado and Amazon biomes. This knowledge will inform the development of effective conservation and management strategies that will help to protect these vital and unique ecosystems.

1.1 Objectives

The main objective of this project is to identify possible causes of deforestation in the Amazon and Cerrado, and describe inter and intra relationships of social and environmental dimensions. This objective is achieved trough the achievement of minor goals:

  1. Indicate possible causes of deforestation trough grounded theory;

  2. Describe social relationships between groups of interest related to the causes, by performing a sociological analysis;

  3. Estimaate the prediction importance and interactions between the causes by statistical regression algorithms;

  4. Identify the convergences and divergences of results;
